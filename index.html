· About the data & caveats
Identity across letters is matched by normalized name; homonyms may merge and spelling variants may split (affects the signatory network most). Signatures are long-format (a person appears once per letter signed), so cross-letter overlap is the subject here. The signatory network draws ties where two people co-signed several letters; the “min co-signed” and “spacing” sliders control density. Names are drawn from public letters.
